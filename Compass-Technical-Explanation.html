?? Compass Mode: How Your Phone Finds North

The Technology Behind It

Your smartphone contains two key sensors that work together to create a digital compass:

  • Magnetometer – Detects Earth's magnetic field (like a traditional compass)
  • Accelerometer – Measures gravity and device orientation

How It Works Without GPS

  • The magnetometer senses Earth's magnetic field to determine magnetic north
  • The accelerometer tells the phone which way is "down" (gravity)
  • By combining these readings, your phone calculates true north and your exact orientation
  • No internet or GPS required – works completely offline using only built-in sensors

Technical Capabilities

  • Accuracy: ±1–3 degrees under ideal conditions
  • Update rate: 10–60 times per second
  • Power usage: Very low (sensors are energy-efficient)
  • Reliability: Works anywhere with Earth's magnetic field

?? Return to Start Navigation: The Hybrid System

How We Guide You Back

This feature combines GPS positioning with compass navigation:

Phase 1: GPS Recording (Requires GPS)

  • GPS records your exact starting coordinates (latitude/longitude)
  • This creates your "home base" – typically accurate within 3–10 meters
  • GPS is only needed initially to set your starting point

Phase 2: Compass Navigation (Works Without GPS)

  1. Current GPS position is compared with your recorded start position
  2. The app calculates:
    • Bearing: The compass direction to travel (0–360°)
    • Distance: How far you are from the start point
  3. Your phone's compass continuously guides you toward that bearing

Why This Works Offline

  • Once the bearing is calculated, you only need the compass to maintain direction
  • Think of it like: “Walk at 245° until you arrive”
  • The compass keeps you on course while the distance decreases as you approach
